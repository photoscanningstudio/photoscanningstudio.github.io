1. Define Your Needs Before You Search

Know what you're looking for:

What are you scanning?¬†Prints only? Slides? Negatives? Albums? Odd sizes? Ensure the service handles your specific media types.

How many items?¬†Get a rough estimate (a shoebox holds roughly 1000-1500 photos) to help compare pricing models.

What's your budget?¬†Services range from budget bulk scanning to premium bespoke handling.

What's your deadline?¬†Do you need them back by a specific date (e.g., for an anniversary, a move)? Turnaround time is crucial.

What quality level do you expect?¬†Are basic scans okay, or do you want high-resolution archival quality with enhancements?

2. Research Potential Services (Local vs. Mail-Away)

Local Services (Like Us in Buffalo!):

Pros:¬†No shipping risks/costs, easier communication, potential for local drop-off/pickup, supporting a local WNY business, often faster turnaround for local clients.

Cons:¬†Fewer options than nationwide mail-away services.

Mail-Away Services:

Pros:¬†More choices, potentially lower advertised base prices (but watch for hidden fees!).

Cons:¬†Risk of photos being lost or damaged in shipping (both ways!), longer overall turnaround time due to shipping, potentially less personal service, harder to resolve issues.

3. Key Questions to Ask Potential Providers:

Don't be afraid to contact services and ask specific questions! Reputable companies will be transparent.

Handling & Safety:

"How do you handle fragile or old photos?"

"Are my photos scanned in-house or sent elsewhere?" (Crucial for security!)

"What precautions do you take to prevent damage?"

"Are you insured?"

Scanning Process & Quality:

"What resolution (DPI) do you scan prints/slides/negatives at?" (Look for 600 DPI for prints, higher for negatives/slides).

"Do you clean photos before scanning?"

"Is color correction or enhancement included, or extra?" (We include enhancements!)

"What file formats do you provide (JPEG, TIFF)?" (TIFF is best for archival, JPEG for sharing).

"Do you provide both original scans and enhanced versions?" (We do!)

Turnaround Time:

"What is your typical turnaround time for a collection my size?" (Ours is typically ~7 days!)

"Do you offer expedited services?"

Pricing & Costs:

"What is your pricing structure (per photo, per box, tiered)?"

"Are there any setup fees or minimum orders?"

"What exactly is included in the price?" (Enhancements? USB drive? Cloud access?)

"Are there extra charges for albums, different sizes, or specific handling?"

"Is return shipping included (for mail-away)?"

Organization & Delivery:

"How will my digital files be organized?" (By folder, matching my batches?)

"How are the digital files delivered?" (USB, Cloud download, DVD?) (We offer USB and Cloud!)

"How long do you keep my files after delivery?"

4. Check Reviews and Testimonials

Look for online reviews (Google, Yelp) and testimonials on the company's website. See what previous customers say about their experience, quality, and customer service. Look for reviews from people in the WNY area if considering a local provider.

5. Consider the "Value" Proposition

Don't just choose based on the absolute lowest price per scan. Consider the overall value:

Is enhancement included (saving you time/money later)?

Is the turnaround time fast (saving you weeks of waiting)?

Is the quality high (saving you from needing rescans)?

Is the service local (saving you shipping stress and supporting the WNY economy)?
