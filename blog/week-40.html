What Qualifies as a "Historical Moment" Photo?

Think broadly:

Major Life Events:¬†Weddings, births, baptisms, graduations, retirements, funerals.

Significant Personal Achievements:¬†Awards, performances, homecomings (military or otherwise).

Why Digitization AND Documentation are Both Crucial:

Digitization = Preservation of the Image:

Creates a stable copy resistant to physical decay (fading, damage).

Protects against catastrophic loss (fire, flood) through backups.

Allows for easy sharing and access without handling fragile originals.

High-resolution scanning (our 600 DPI standard)¬†captures fine details potentially important for historical analysis (e.g., reading signs in the background, identifying clothing details).

Documentation = Preservation of the Meaning:

Identifies the "5 Ws":¬†Who is in the photo? What is happening? When was it taken? Where is it (specific location, e.g., "Lafayette Square, Buffalo")? Why was the photo taken/What is the significance?

Connects to Broader History:¬†Explains how the personal moment relates to larger events or trends.

Prevents Misinterpretation:¬†Ensures future viewers understand the context accurately.

Adds Value for Research:¬†Provides crucial data for family historians or even local WNY historical researchers.

Leverage the power of digital files!

Descriptive File Names:¬†Include date, event, location (e.g.,¬†1977-01-29_Blizzard_Aftermath_Maple_St_Kenmore_001.jpg).

Organize Thematically/Chronologically:¬†Group photos from the same event or era in dedicated folders.

Create Supporting Documents:¬†For particularly significant photos or collections, consider writing a separate document detailing the history and linking it to the relevant image files.
